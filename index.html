About Me

Where science meets learning

I am an MSc Chemistry graduate with a background in teaching, school counseling, and educational technology development. My work sits at the intersection of deep scientific knowledge and the challenge of making that knowledge genuinely accessible to learners.

As a Chemistry Teacher and School Counselor, I have worked directly with students to understand where learning breaks down — and I build web tools that address those specific gaps. Every project I create starts from a real educational problem, not just a technical exercise.

I develop interactive science applications using modern web technologies, combining my domain expertise in chemistry, materials science, and biology with strong visual design to create tools that students, teachers, and researchers actually want to use.

ChemSphere 3D

Problem solved: Students struggle to visualise 3D molecular geometry from 2D textbook diagrams.

An interactive browser-based platform that lets students rotate, zoom, and explore molecular structures in real time — no software installation required. Designed specifically for chemistry students who need spatial understanding to ace exams.

MOFFIT

Problem solved: MOF crystal structures are nearly impossible to grasp from static diagrams in research papers.

An interactive visualization platform for Metal-Organic Framework structures — porous crystalline materials critical to carbon capture and gas storage research. Built for chemistry students and researchers who need spatial intuition for advanced materials.

🔬 Researchers 📖 Postgrad Students
JavaScript MOF Structures Materials Science

NanoLit

Problem solved: Navigating dense nanoscience literature is overwhelming for students new to research.

A scientific literature exploration tool that helps students and early-career researchers navigate nanoscience publications with intelligent filtering. Reduces the barrier to entry for evidence-based learning.
